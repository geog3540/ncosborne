Hello!

Below, you will find what I worked on during the Spring of 2025 in my Geographic Visualization class. I used HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and mapping libraries and tools such as CartoDB, Leaflet, and D3. Clicking a map will expand it, and clicking the map title will take you to its Observable Notebook. There, you can read my full spatial analysis. Thank you for visiting!

Disability Rate by U.S. County

This is a choropleth map of disability rate respective to every U.S. county, according to the census in 2000.

Amtrak Stations by Ridership

This is a proportional symbol map of U.S. Amtrak Stations by Ridership in 2022.

Disability Rate and High School Graduation Rate, West Virginia

This map depicts the disability rate and high school graduation rate respective to each West Virginian county, in 2000.

Isarithmic Mapping of Yearly Total Precipitation

Isarithmic Mapping of Yearly Total Precipitation by Caglar Koylu.

Flowmap

This is a flowmap depicting changes in family migration between 1887 - 1924.

Final Project: Cape Canaveral Launches, 1954 - 2011

For my final project, I plan to explore the landscape of the most active space port in the country: Cape Canaveral, FL. This is a proportional symbol map of launches from deactivated ports.
