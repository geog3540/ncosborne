Flow Map, 1887 - 1924
Net Flow, Total Flow, and Flow Paths of Families

Places that recieve the most flows are in the Midwest and South, such as Texas, Oklahoma, and Missouri. Places that send the most flows are also Midwestern states, like Iowa, Arkansas, and Missouri. The strongest flows go towards the southwest, towards Texas and Oklahoma from the upper Midwest. There are also many states flowing into Texas, though Texans were moving at high numbers into Oklahoma and minimally back into Arkansas. There were many small flows into Ohio, and deep mutual interchanges happening in Kansas, Iowa, and Missouri. It was uncommon for there to be states skipping over their neighbors to reach a further state directly, with the exception of Tennessee and Alabama to Texas. In relation to the node symbols, the strongest flows enter into states with the largest total flow. This makes sense, as the total flow is a raw count number, so the strongest flows would introduce the largest population changes. In relation to the choropleth base map, the flows show the migration pattern out of the East and South. All of these states, with the exception of Florida, are in a net negative flow. The largest flows go from East to West, and this is where we see the change in states from negative to positive flows.
